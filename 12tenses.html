The Twelve Verb Tenses

A complete reference guide — structure, usage, signal words, and common errors for French speakers.

Present

1
Simple Present
subject + base verb (+ ‑s/‑es for he/she/it)
▾
Primary Uses
Habits & routines — She studies every morning.
General truths & facts — Water boils at 100°C.
Fixed schedules — The exam starts at 9 a.m.
Signal Words
alwaysusuallyoftenevery dayneversometimes
⚠ French Speaker Trap
✗ I am eating breakfast every day.
✓ I eat breakfast every day.
Habitual actions require simple present, never continuous.
2
Present Continuous
am/is/are + verb‑ing
▾
Primary Uses
Actions happening right now — I am studying this tense.
Temporary situations — She is living in New York this semester.
Confirmed future plans — We are meeting tomorrow.
Signal Words
nowright nowat the momentcurrentlythis week
⚠ Stative Verbs — Never Continuous
know · believe · want · need · love · hate · own · understand · see · hear
✗ I am knowing the answer.
✓ I know the answer.
3
Present Perfect
have/has + past participle
▾
Primary Uses
Past action with present relevance — I have lost my passport. (still missing)
Life experiences, no specific time — She has visited Japan.
Action started in past, continuing now — I have lived here for five years.
Signal Words
alreadyyeteverneverforsincejustrecently
⚠ Your Highest-Risk Error (passé composé confusion)
Rule: Specific past time = Simple Past. No specific time = Present Perfect.
✗ I have finished the test yesterday.
✓ I finished the test yesterday.
✗ I already saw this film.
✓ I have already seen this film.
4
Present Perfect Continuous
have/has been + verb‑ing
▾
Primary Uses
Ongoing action from past to now, emphasizing duration — I have been studying English for three years.
Recent activity with visible present result — She has been crying. (her eyes are red)
Signal Words
forsinceall dayall weekhow long
Key Distinction vs. Present Perfect
→ I have read that book. — focus on the result (finished)
→ I have been reading that book. — focus on the ongoing process

Past

5
Simple Past
subject + past form (regular: +‑ed / irregular: varies)
▾
Primary Uses
Completed action at a specific past time — He graduated in 2021.
Sequence of past events — She woke up, showered, and left.
Signal Words
yesterdaylast yearin 2020agothenwhen
Key Irregular Verbs
BaseSimple PastPast Participle
gowentgone
writewrotewritten
speakspokespoken
seesawseen
taketooktaken
6
Past Continuous
was/were + verb‑ing
▾
Primary Uses
Action in progress at a specific past moment — At 8 p.m., I was writing my essay.
Background action interrupted by another — I was studying when she called.
Signal Words
whilewhenat that momentall day
Key Pattern
While + past continuous + , + simple past
While I was cooking, the phone rang.
7
Past Perfect
had + past participle
▾
Primary Uses
The action that happened first when narrating two past events — When I arrived, the lecture had already started.
Unmet past conditions — She had never seen snow before she moved to Canada.
Signal Words
alreadybeforewhenby the timeafter
⚠ Do Not Overuse
Use past perfect only when the sequence of two past events must be made unambiguous. When before or after already makes the order clear, simple past is often acceptable.
8
Past Perfect Continuous
had been + verb‑ing
▾
Primary Uses
An ongoing past action that preceded another past event, emphasizing duration — He had been waiting for two hours before the doctor saw him.
They had been arguing all night before they finally agreed.
Signal Words
forsincebeforewhenby the time

Future

9
Simple Future
will + base verb  |  be going to + base verb
▾
Will — Uses
Spontaneous decisions — I'll help you with that.
Predictions based on opinion — I think technology will change education.
Promises & offers — I will call you tomorrow.
Be Going To — Uses
Pre-decided plans — I am going to apply for a scholarship.
Predictions based on present evidence — Look at those clouds — it is going to rain.
Signal Words
tomorrownext weeksoonin the future
10
Future Continuous
will be + verb‑ing
▾
Primary Uses
Action that will be in progress at a specific future moment — At this time tomorrow, I will be sitting the TOEFL exam.
This time next year, she will be living in Boston.
Signal Words
at this time tomorrowthis time next yearstill
11
Future Perfect
will have + past participle
▾
Primary Uses
Action completed before a specific future point — By December, I will have finished all my applications.
She will have graduated before the project ends.
Signal Words
byby the timebeforeby next year
12
Future Perfect Continuous
will have been + verb‑ing
▾
Primary Uses
Duration of an action still ongoing at a future point — By next month, I will have been studying English for two years.
When she retires, she will have been teaching for thirty years.
Signal Words
byforwhenby the time
